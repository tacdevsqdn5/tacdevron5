    TACD&E5, Silver Squadron

    TACD&E5, specifically Silver Squadron, is part of the Naval Special Warfare Development Group, commonly known as SEAL Team 6 or DEVGRU. This elite unit of the U.S. Navy SEALs is known for conducting highly sensitive and classified missions, including counter-terrorism, hostage rescue, and direct action operations.

    To become a part of this prestigious unit, one must follow a rigorous career path, starting with earning the NEC (Navy Enlisted Classification) codes for Special Warfare Operator (SO) or Special Warfare Boat Operator (SB).

    Special Warfare Operator (SO): This designation is achieved through extensive training and proves the operator's expertise in SEAL operations, including combat diving, land warfare, and advanced marksmanship. SOs are the backbone of the SEAL teams, known for their versatility and capability in various mission profiles.

    Special Warfare Boat Operator (SB): SBs specialize in maritime operations, mastering the use of special boats for clandestine missions. They are essential for supporting SEAL teams, providing critical maritime mobility and tactical support.

    Operators from both career paths undergo further specialized training and assessment to qualify for DEVGRU. This elite unit demands the highest level of proficiency, adaptability, and teamwork, ensuring mission success in the most challenging and high-stakes scenarios. The path to Silver Squadron is paved with dedication, rigorous training, and an unwavering commitment to excellence.

    160th Special Operations Aviation Regiment (A)

    The 160th Special Operations Aviation Regiment (Airborne), commonly referred to as the 160th SOAR or the "Night Stalkers," is a specialized unit of the United States Army. This regiment is known for its proficiency in nighttime operations and plays a crucial role in supporting special operations forces.

    Within the 160th SOAR, career paths for officers typically involve the Army MOS (Military Occupational Specialty) code 15A for Aviation Officer.

    Aviation Officer (15A): As a 15A, an Aviation Officer is responsible for overseeing and coordinating aviation operations, including mission planning, flight management, and ensuring the readiness of aircraft. They play a vital role in leading aerial missions, often in high-risk and nighttime environments, and work closely with special operations forces to provide precise and effective air support.

    The role demands exceptional leadership, tactical expertise, and the ability to operate under challenging conditions. Officers in this specialty undergo extensive training to meet the high standards of the 160th SOAR, ensuring their success in one of the Army's most elite aviation units.

    24th Special Tactics Squadron (STS)

    The 24th Special Tactics Squadron (24th STS) is an elite unit within the U.S. Air Force, part of the Air Force Special Operations Command (AFSOC). This squadron is tasked with providing combat control, pararescue, and tactical air control support to U.S. special operations forces.

    Within the 24th STS, personnel typically hold the following Air Force Specialty Codes (AFSCs):

    Combat Control (1C2X1): Combat Controllers (CCT) are highly trained in a range of specialized skills, including air traffic control, demolition, and advanced combat tactics. Their primary mission is to establish air control and coordinate precision air support in denied or hostile environments. CCTs are known for their expertise in rapidly setting up and managing airfields, guiding aircraft into contested areas, and providing critical support to ground forces.

    Pararescue (1T2X1): Pararescue specialists, also known as PJs, are experts in combat search and rescue (CSAR) operations. They are trained in emergency medical treatment, high-altitude rescue techniques, and tactical operations. PJs are often called upon to recover injured personnel from the battlefield and provide life-saving medical care under challenging conditions. Their training emphasizes versatility, endurance, and the ability to operate effectively in both hostile and austere environments.

    Both CCTs and PJs in the 24th STS undergo rigorous training to meet the demanding standards of the squadron, ensuring they are capable of performing their high-stakes missions with precision and excellence.

    24th Special Tactics Squadron (STS) Pipeline Overview

    The 24th Special Tactics Squadron (STS) pipeline is designed to develop highly skilled operators. This training encompasses both Combat Control (1C2X1) and Pararescue (1T2X1) roles, ensuring personnel are equipped to handle diverse and demanding scenarios.

    Combat Control (1C2X1) Training Modules

    • Combat Control Training Course (CCTC): Includes physical conditioning, parachuting, demolitions, and combat skills.
    • Advanced Air Traffic Control Training: Focused on air traffic control and special operations tactics.
    • Ongoing Training: Continuous education in survival, evasion, resistance, and escape (SERE) techniques.

    Pararescue (1T2X1) Training Modules

    • Pararescue Training Course (PJTC): Comprehensive training in advanced medical skills, survival, scuba diving, and parachuting.
    • Specialized Tactical Training: Includes advanced medical procedures and tactical rescue operations.
    • Continuous Training: Regular updates on medical advancements, operational tactics, and survival skills.
